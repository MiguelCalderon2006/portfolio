Leadership Experience

It is a late summer night; I am coding a Batman-themed word guessing mini-game when my little brother who was up playing video games takes notice of the ASCII art on my screen. He was captivated that while he was playing a game, I was creating my own. That night we worked on the game together bouncing ideas off each other while I debugged the code. This is the night I realized my role as my little brother's guide and mentor in the world of programming.

I started teaching him the basics of programming and organizing weekly tutoring sessions with him. As we got into harder concepts, I could see the spark fading away, using his free time on video games rather than programming. Knowing I had to rekindle his interest in programming I took an innovative approach. Rather than following a boring rigid course I decided to make things interesting, making mini games and having him try and replicate it using his own code. I also had to pre-plan tutoring sessions to make sure I communicated everything to him clearly. This approach was much more successful because it brought your ideas to life. Being a mentor in this situation required me to be flexible and creative in my teaching.

Driven by the interesting aspects of programming, my younger brother followed suit, expressing a curious desire to kickstart his own programming journey by taking advantage of dual enrollment while in high school. I remain actively involved in helping him balance the workload of both high school and college classes, ensuring him the support and guidance needed to excel.

Mentoring my little brother has taught me that leadership does not always imply the use of force or rigidity, but instead compassion, empathy, and patience. I am now well-equipped to apply those leadership values when tutoring my peers. These experiences have reinforced the idea that a good leader is a role model, and in order to help those around me, I must continue to better myself to become a positive influence to others.
